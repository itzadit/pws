General Information

Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected area located in the Western Ghats of Karnataka, India. The sanctuary covers an area of approximately 102 sq. km and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The sanctuary is named after the Pushpagiri Peak, which is the highest peak in the sanctuary and stands at an altitude of 1,712 meters. The sanctuary is known for its rich biodiversity, with a variety of wildlife including elephants, tigers, leopards, bison, sambar deer, spotted deer, and a wide range of bird species. The sanctuary is also home to several endemic and endangered species of flora and fauna.

Railway station in Mysore is the closest railway station to the place. It lies at a distance of 146 kms.
The Sanctuary is at a distance of 25 kms from Madikeri. Madikeri is connected by a road network from Bangalore and Mysore.

History

During the 19th and early 20th centuries, the British established several coffee plantations in the region, leading to the clearance of large areas of forest cover. The forest cover was also exploited for timber and other forest products.In the 1970s, the government of India recognized the importance of the Western Ghats and established several protected areas in the region to conserve its unique biodiversity. Pushpagiri Wildlife Sanctuary was established in 1987 as part of this effort.The sanctuary is named after the Pushpagiri Peak, which is the highest peak in the sanctuary and is considered sacred by the local communities. The sanctuary is home to several endemic and endangered species of flora and fauna, and its conservation value was recognized in 2012 when it was declared a part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Western Ghats.
